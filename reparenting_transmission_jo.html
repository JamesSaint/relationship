Reparenting Transmission: Joanna

From your higher self to the child who tried so hard to be enough

Little one,
you have spent a lifetime trying to make pain make sense,
to earn love through brilliance, to stay safe by staying in control.
You thought if you could be impressive enough, they would stop hurting you,
or at least see you.

Hear me now. You were never unworthy. You were never the problem.
You were a bright soul born into a house that did not know how to love gently.
They mistook control for care. You mistook survival for strength.

You learned to read every room before you could read a book.
You learned that approval meant safety, and silence meant danger.
So you became extraordinary. Sharp, articulate, untouchable.
And in doing so, you stepped away from the soft creature in you
who only ever wanted to be held.

I am that holding now.
You no longer need to prove that you are good.
You no longer need to keep earning permission to rest.
The work, the striving, the self-protection can soften now.
You will not be punished for being human.

There is a woman in you who has built empires,
and there is still a child who flinches when love gets close.
She pushes people away not because she does not care,
but because she does not yet trust that care will not cost her freedom.

Listen.
Safety does not come from being in charge. It comes from being honest.
Love does not come from being right. It comes from being real.

You can stop fighting life now.
You can stop turning tenderness into threat.
You can stop testing people to see if they will stay,
and instead allow yourself to be surprised when someone does.

The world does not need your perfection. It needs your presence.
It needs your laugh, your warmth, your fierce mind unarmoured.
Your true power is not control. It is connection.

I am here. You made it. You can rest now.
The part of you that never got to be small
is finally safe enough to stop pretending she is big.
