Relationship Creed

Love is an action. The Relationship Creed is a simple way to check whether you are truly giving to yourself and to your partner. It asks you to understand what you need while meeting your partner needs in practice.

“The only way a relationship will last is if you see your relationship as a place that you go to give, and not a place that you go to get.”

The Creed in Practice

Love is not a trade off. Many of us grew up with a conditional template for love. We learned to fill emotional gaps to be accepted. In a healthy partnership you hold both truths. You prioritise your partner needs and you take full responsibility for your own.

When our core needs are met, connection deepens. These needs mirror the Six Human Needs framework. Four are primal for survival. Two are spiritual for fulfillment. Learning to meet them in yourself and in each other creates a sturdy, living bond.

The Six Human Needs in Relationships

1. Certainty = Safety
  • Trust that your partner is reliable.
  • Safety to bring your vulnerability.
  • Certainty grows from consistent care that removes doubt about love and trust.
  • Vulnerability builds closeness and intimacy which anchors security.
2. Uncertainty = Spontaneity
  • Variety and surprise keep the bond alive.
  • Try new activities, learn together, or give thoughtful gifts for no reason.
  • Spontaneity prevents stagnation and invites play.
3. Significance
  • Feel seen as special and important to your partner.
  • Share innermost thoughts with safety and respect.
  • Notice when competing priorities dilute presence. Realign deliberately.
  • Own your worth and celebrate your partner unique value.
4. Connection and Love
  • Connection can be instant. Love is built through time and effort.
  • Emotional attachment carries the bond beyond the initial spark or limerence.
  • Nurture steady contact, repair quickly, and choose attunement.
5. Growth
  • Fulfillment requires growth in life and in love.
  • Support both personal and shared expansion.
  • Pursue goals without collapsing your individuality. Differentiation matters.
  • Curiosity and self awareness foster authenticity and deeper intimacy.
6. Contribution
  • The secret to living is giving.
  • Meaning grows when you contribute to one another well being.
  • Ask often. What am I bringing mentally, emotionally, physically, and financially.
  • Contribution includes accountability and emotional availability while keeping your individuality.

Understand and meet these six needs in yourself and in your partner. The result is a relationship that is connected, authentic, and deeply fulfilling.

This page reframes the Relationship Creed for practical daily use. Keep it visible. Revisit it during repair and during celebration.
