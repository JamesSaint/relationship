Four Horsemen Relationship Handout

Watch. Understand. Practice. Repair.

Source video embedded for reference.

Overview

The Four Horsemen are four patterns that predict relationship distress when left unchecked. Learn to notice them in real time and apply the antidotes. Small shifts practiced often change the climate of the relationship.

  • Criticism targets your partner character instead of the specific behavior.
  • Contempt shows disrespect or superiority through sarcasm, eye rolling, mockery, or moral high ground.
  • Defensiveness blocks responsibility taking and fuels escalation.
  • Stonewalling is shutting down or checking out when overwhelmed.

Each has an antidote you can practice. Use the scripts below until they become natural.

Horsemen and Antidotes

Criticism

Global attack or blame. Often starts with you and you statements.

Antidote: Gentle start up. Describe what happened, how you feel, and what you need next.

Example shift: Instead of You never listen, try I felt unheard in that call. I need five minutes with your full attention to debrief.

  • Observe the behavior. No labels. No character attacks.
  • Name your feeling with I feel language.
  • State a specific, doable request.
Contempt

Disrespect through sarcasm, ridicule, eye rolling, or moral superiority.

Antidote: Build appreciation and respect. Express needs without put downs.

Example shift: From Really, you messed this up again to I appreciate how much you care. I need us to check the plan together before we send it.

  • Practice daily appreciations. Three specifics each day.
  • Replace sarcasm with direct requests.
  • Notice body language. Relax jaw and shoulders.
Defensiveness

Counterattacking or victim posture instead of owning your part.

Antidote: Take responsibility for even a small piece of the problem.

Example shift: You are blaming me becomes You are right that I missed the reminder. I will set an alarm and follow through today.

  • Listen for the kernel of truth.
  • Own your behavior without excuses.
  • State your plan for repair or prevention.
Stonewalling

Shutting down when flooded. Heart rate is up and the nervous system is overloaded.

Antidote: Self soothe. Take a short break and return when calm.

Example script: I notice I am getting overwhelmed. I need a twenty minute break. I will come back at 5.30 to continue.

  • Call a pause. Minimum twenty minutes. No rumination.
  • Regulate. Breathe, walk, stretch, cold water, music.
  • Return and re engage at the agreed time.

Repair Scripts

  • Gentle start up: I felt [feeling] when [specific event]. I need [clear request].
  • Owning your part: You are right that I [behavior]. I will [repair or next step].
  • Appreciation swap: One thing I appreciate about you is [specific]. Thank you for [action].
  • Timeout: I want to get this right. I notice I am getting flooded. Can we pause for twenty minutes and continue at [time].
  • After a miss: I see how my words landed. That was not my intention. I get why you felt [feeling]. Here is what I will do differently next time.

Practice Plan

  1. Daily. Name one appreciation. Keep it specific and behavioral.
  2. Weekly. Choose one recurring friction point. Rehearse the gentle start up together.
  3. During conflict. Spot the horseman in play. Use the matching antidote script.
  4. After conflict. Debrief for five minutes. What worked. What to change.

Self Soothing

Flooding reduces perspective and empathy. Use short, physical resets that bring your nervous system back within range.

  • Box breathing. Inhale four. Hold four. Exhale four. Hold four. Repeat for two minutes.
  • Slow walk. Count steps up to twenty and restart.
  • Progressive muscle release. Tense and release fists, jaw, shoulders.
  • Rinse wrists with cool water for one minute.

Reflection Prompts

  • Which horseman shows up most for me. What is the early signal that it is coming.
  • What appreciation have I not voiced this week.
  • What request could replace my next complaint.
  • How will I know I am flooded. What is my planned timeout script.

Quick Checklist

  • Describe behavior. No labels. No you never or you always.
  • Use I feel to name one clear feeling.
  • Make one specific request that can be done today.
  • Offer one appreciation before or after the request.
  • Pause when flooded and return at an agreed time.
