Parting Ways

Jo,

From the very start I was clear that I didnt have money to bring into this relationship. I stretched myself even in those early days because I wanted to meet you and see what was possible between us.

This past year has cost me more than I could sustain. Ive admitted Ive been surviving on 500 a month while pouring my time, energy, and resources into keeping us moving driving everywhere, covering our household costs, carrying quiet shame as my health declined and my finances thinned to breaking point. Meanwhile, youve had the safety of a full salary and benefits.

I want you to know this: when I drove up to move in with you, I was fully committed. I wanted us to work, not just for love but for the stability and shared life we spoke of. Thats why its been so painful to reach the point where I had to say on Sunday that the relationship is over. I gave more than I could, and I cant carry it any further.

I dont want to argue over numbers or sacrifices. What I am asking is that you consider making a parting contribution not as a transaction, but as recognition of what Ive shouldered and to help me move forward.

We also need to arrange for you to collect your things, so we can close this cleanly for both of us.

James

James Saint 2025
