Parting Ways

Jo,

From the very start I was clear that I didn’t have money to bring into this relationship. I stretched myself even in those early days because I wanted to meet you and see what was possible between us.

This past year has cost me more than I could sustain. I’ve admitted I’ve been surviving on £500 a month while pouring my time, energy, and resources into keeping us moving … driving everywhere, covering our household costs, carrying quiet shame as my health declined and my finances thinned to breaking point. Meanwhile, you’ve had the safety of a full salary and benefits.

I want you to know this: when I drove up to move in with you, I was fully committed. I wanted us to work, not just for love but for the stability and shared life we spoke of. That’s why it’s been so painful to reach the point where I had to say on Sunday that the relationship is over. I gave more than I could, and I can’t carry it any further.

I don’t want to argue over numbers or sacrifices. What I am asking is that you consider making a parting contribution … not as a transaction, but as recognition of what I’ve shouldered and to help me move forward.

We also need to arrange for you to collect your things, so we can close this cleanly for both of us.

James
